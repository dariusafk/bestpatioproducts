The ICC Certification: Why It Actually Matters

Here's something most homeowners don't know. StruXure is the only pergola company in America with ICC certification. ICC stands for the International Code Council, and their certification means an independent third party has verified that StruXure's engineering meets or exceeds building code requirements.

Why does this matter? Three reasons.

First, permits. If you live in a jurisdiction that requires a building permit for a pergola (and many do), ICC certification makes the process dramatically easier. Your building inspector sees the ICC stamp and knows the engineering has already been validated. No back-and-forth. No additional engineering reports. No delays.

Second, insurance. Some homeowner's insurance policies require structures to meet code. ICC certification gives you documentation that holds up if you ever need to file a claim.

Third, peace of mind. When a company voluntarily submits to independent verification, it tells you something about their confidence in the product. Nobody with a questionable product invites the ICC to come look under the hood.

No other residential pergola brand has this. Not Equinox. Not Hanso. Not Pergolux. Not The Luxury Pergola. Just StruXure. That's a genuine competitive advantage, and I want to be clear about that.

The Dealer Markup Problem Nobody Talks About

I want to be careful here because I work with StruXure dealers and I respect their business. But my job on this site is to give you the honest picture, so here it is.

StruXure's distribution model has four layers. The factory makes the product. A regional distributor buys from the factory. A local dealer buys from the distributor. And the dealer's installer puts it on your patio. Each layer needs to make a profit. Each layer adds overhead, insurance, warehousing, showroom costs, and marketing expenses to the price you pay.

This is why a product made from the same family of aluminum alloys ends up costing 7 to 12 times more than a direct-to-consumer alternative. It's not because the aluminum costs 7 to 12 times more. It's because you're paying for the showroom in a nice part of town, the sales rep's commission, the distributor's warehouse, and the factory's margin to the distributor. Five hands in the supply chain, and each one takes a cut.

Direct-to-consumer brands like Hanso eliminated those layers entirely. Factory to your door. One margin instead of four. That's not cutting corners on quality. That's cutting corners on middlemen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is StruXure worth the price?

If you're in the top 10% of pergola buyers (full outdoor renovation over $150K, or you simply must have the best regardless of cost), yes. For everyone else, the Hanso Horizon delivers 90% of the experience at roughly 10% of the price. The structural performance gap has narrowed dramatically in the last two years.

Can I install a StruXure myself?

No. StruXure requires professional installation through their certified dealer network. This ensures proper engineering and protects your warranty, but it also means you can't save money on labor.

How long does a StruXure last?

With proper maintenance, 20 to 30+ years easily. The powder coating holds up well, the aluminum doesn't corrode, and the mechanical components are well-engineered. The lifetime structural warranty backs that up.

Why is StruXure so expensive compared to Hanso?

Distribution model. StruXure goes through manufacturer, distributor, dealer, and installer, with each layer adding margin. Hanso ships factory-direct. You're paying for the same category of aluminum, but through very different supply chains.

Does the Hanso Horizon really match StruXure on structural performance?

On raw specs, the Horizon is very competitive. It uses 6063-T6 aluminum (170 MPa yield strength), handles 165 mph winds (IBC 2024 validated with SAP2000 finite element analysis), and manages 60 PSF snow loads. StruXure has the edge on ICC certification, custom sizing, and louver rotation range. But the structural performance gap does not justify a 7-12x price gap.
