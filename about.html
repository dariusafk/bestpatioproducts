Owner of Harmon Outdoor Living. 15+ years installing pergolas, patios, and outdoor kitchens across the Carolinas, Virginia, and Georgia. This site is where I document what I've learned from 300+ installs.

Why I Started This Site

I've been doing outdoor structure work since 2009. Started with decks, moved into patios, and eventually pergolas became the bulk of my business around 2016 when louvered pergolas really took off in the Southeast.

Over the years, I've installed products from pretty much every brand you can buy online. Some of them blew me away. Some of them made me want to apologize to my clients. And the frustrating thing was that there was nowhere online where a regular homeowner could get a straight answer about which ones were actually good.

Every "review" site was either written by someone who clearly never touched the product, or it was so loaded with marketing speak that you couldn't trust any of it. So in 2022, I started writing up my actual experiences. What's easy to install. What holds up after a Carolina hurricane season. What my clients are happy with two years later. What I'd never install again.

That's what this site is. No lab testing. No calipers. Just 15 years of hands-on experience and a phone full of job site photos.

How I Review Products

My reviews are based on three things:

  1. Installation experience. How long does it actually take? What tools do you need? Are the instructions clear? Can two people handle it or do you need a crew? I've installed most of these products multiple times, so I know what the second and third install teaches you that the first one doesn't.
  2. Build quality feel. When I pick up a beam or a louver, I can tell in two seconds if it's going to hold up. Wall thickness, joint quality, the way pieces fit together. You can't fake structural integrity. Either the parts feel solid or they don't.
  3. Client happiness. This is the one that matters most. I stay in touch with my clients. I know which pergolas are still getting compliments after two years and which ones needed warranty calls after six months. That real-world feedback is worth more than any spec sheet.

I rate products on a simple five-tier scale: Best in Class, Strong Buy, Solid Choice, Proceed with Caution, and Hard Pass. No complicated formulas. Just my honest assessment based on everything above.

What I'm Not

I'm not an engineer. I don't have a materials science degree. I can't run finite element analysis on a louver profile. If you want that level of technical depth, there are other resources out there.

What I am is a guy who's spent 15 years putting these things together with his hands, watching them survive (or not survive) real weather, and listening to real homeowners tell me what they love and what they regret. That perspective is hard to find online, and it's what I try to bring to every review I write.
